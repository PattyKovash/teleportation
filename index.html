TELEPORTATION &

QUANTUM PHYSICS

Teleportation has been successfully completed in real life. No joke.

Through "spooky action at a distance", science fiction became science fact.

Spooky action at a what?! Hey, those were Einstein's words, not mine.

If you know the phenomenon behind this breakthrough,

you may agree with such a description. Einstein was referring to...

Quantum entanglement. Stay with me now. It's going to get weird.

Imagine 2 objects. We can decide if they'll be either blue or red.

These two objects are bound to eachother. They're partners.

BUT until we decide, both randomly & constantly change between red & blue.

Let's leave one object on earth & send the other 50 million light years away to space.

Yes, 50 MILLION light years. But, it could be 50 trillion or quadrillion and beyond.

Ok, now we're ready to decide their fate.

Let's make the object on earth blue. Now let's take a look at the object in space.

Wait...what?! The object in space is red, but we didn't decide on it's color yet...

Ok, let's redo this. 2 new objects please.

We'll start with the object in space this time. Let's make it blue.

Now let's go back to the object on earth. Do you know where this is going...?

Hmm...the object on earth is red...even though we didn't decide on its color yet.

And however many times we repeat this experiment, the same thing will happen.

When the fate of one object is decided,

the fate of the other is instantaneously decided to be the opposite.

Make one blue, the other becomes red.

Or make one spin clockwise and the other will have a counterclockwise spin.

Regardless of the distance, the transfer of information is seemingly instant.

This state change of the partner object happens faster than the speed of light.

Faster than the speed of light? But isn't that impossible? Nope, not in quantum physics.
